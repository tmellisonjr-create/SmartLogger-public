# SmartLogger-public
It's been a dream of mine to create a logging program that fully integrates with my FLEX-6700, Flex-8600M, Auroa AU-520, and PGXL. Finially with a little help from my good buddy Claude, that dream has come to fruition with SmartLogger.

SmartLogger is a full-featured ham radio logging program made to leverage the SmartSDR radio hardware by interfacing directly to a FLEX-6000, FLEX-8000, or Aurora radio.  It is SmatLink an multiFlex aware, and integrates with the 4O3A PGXL.

A complete overview of everything SmartLogger does is found in the Feature Help file — from rapid manual QSO entry to automatic WSJT-X logging, FlexRadio slice tracking, SmartLink support, and live radio telemetry, LoTW, and eQSL.cc upload and sync, logbook ADIF import, paper QSL card tracking, DXCC entity tracking, space weather, the interactive Grid Map of worked squares, and the Grayline / Spots Globe with real-time 3-D day/night shading and live DX spot visualization.

<strong>Getting Started:</strong> After downloading and installing the application on your Windows 10 or Windows 11 PC, navigate to the <i>Help->Features</i> menu selection to familiarize yourself with SmartLoggers features.  

Then select <i>Help->Getting Started</i>, which will guide you through setting up an Operator (that you be you!) and connecting to a radio.  

Next, take a look at the <i>Help->Radio Setup</i>, which covers how to discover your radio and log into a SmartLink account to use remote radios.  As a SmartSDR user, the concepts should be second nature, but this will familiarize yourself on how to do it using SmartLogger.

Then check out the <i>Help->Entering a QSO</i>, and you should be on your way!
